    Dream Machine turns your smartphone into a psychedelic drug. The app is based on an ancient method of creating visual hallucinations through flickering light at a specific speed. This technique has been used by William S. Burroughs, Iggy Pop, and most notably Nostradamus. It is thought that Nostradamus made many of his future predictions as a result of hallucinations experienced through the Dream Machine technique.

1. Position yourself.

Before viewing, lay down in a comfortable position so that bodily sensations won't disturb you during the experience. It's most ideal to place your phone inside of a Google Cardboard headset. If you don't have one, simply position the phone (or other device screen) as close to your eyes as possible.

2. Allow the eyes to unfocus.

After starting the app, with the screen in position, gradually relax your eyes allowing them to go out of focus. If you're having difficulty, pretend that you are looking through the screen at something far away.

3. Be open to the experience.

The amount of time varies for users, but after about 5-10 minutes, you will begin to experience visual distortions. The first visuals you will see will likely have a phosphene flavor. As the session progresses, the visuals will become more and more intense, as your brain enters a rate of functioning that resides halfway between dreaming and waking.

Seizure Warning

A very small percentage of people may experience a seizure when exposed to certain visual images, including flashing lights or patterns that may appear in video games. Even people who have no history of seizures or epilepsy may have an undiagnosed condition that can cause these “photosensitive epileptic seizures” while watching video games. These seizures may have a variety of symptoms, including lightheadedness, altered vision, eye or face twitching, jerking or shaking of arms or legs, disorientation, confusion, or momentary loss of awareness. Seizures may also cause loss of consciousness or convulsions that can lead to injury from falling down or striking nearby objects. Immediately stop playing and consult a doctor if you experience any of these symptoms. Parents should watch for or ask their children about the above symptoms. Children and teenagers are more likely than adults to experience these seizures.
